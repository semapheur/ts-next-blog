# Dimension symbols

- time: $\mathrm{T}$
- length: $\mathrm{L}$
- mass: $\mathrm{M}$
- electric current: $\mathrm{I}$
- absolute temperature: $\Theta$
- amount of substance: $N$
- luminous intensity: $J$

The dimension of a quantity $\mathrm{Q}$ is given by

$$
  \dim(Q) \mathrm{T}^a \mathrm{L}^b \mathrm{M}^c \mathrm{I}^d \Theta^d \mathrm{N}^f \mathrm{J}^g
$$

where $a, b, c, d, e, f, g$ are dimensional exponents. The following types of quantities are distinguished
- geometric quantity: $b \neq 0$ and $a = c = d = e = f = g = 0$
- kinematic quantity: $a \neq 0$, $b \neq 0$ and $c = d = e = f = g = 0$
- dynamic quantity: $a \neq 0$, $b \neq 0$, $c \neq 0$ and $d = e = f = g = 0$
- dimensionless quantity (dimension one): $a = b = c = d = e = f = g = 0$
